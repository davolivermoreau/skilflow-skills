# Cold Outreach Email

Writes personalized cold outreach emails that get responses — because they don't sound like cold outreach emails.

## What this skill does

Generates cold emails that are specific, short, and human. No templates that scream "I sent this to 500 people."

## How to use

Provide:
- Who you're reaching out to (name, role, company)
- Why you're reaching out (specific reason, not generic)
- What you want (one clear ask)
- Any personal connection or context

Then say "write a cold outreach email using this skill."

## The framework

**Subject line:** Specific enough to be curious, short enough to read on mobile. Never "Quick question" or "Following up."

**Opening (1 sentence):** Something specific to them — not a compliment, a genuine observation. Something only someone who actually looked at their work would know.

**The bridge (1-2 sentences):** Connect their world to why you're reaching out. Make the relevance obvious without over-explaining.

**The ask (1 sentence):** One thing. Specific. Low friction. "15-minute call" not "your thoughts on a potential partnership opportunity."

**Sign-off:** Short. No "Looking forward to hearing from you."

---

## Rules

- Under 100 words in the body
- One ask per email
- No attachments on first contact
- If you can't say something specific about them, don't send it
- If they don't respond in 5 days, one follow-up only

## What not to do

- "I came across your profile and was impressed by..."
- "I'd love to pick your brain..."
- "We help companies like yours..."
- Three paragraphs explaining your product before the ask

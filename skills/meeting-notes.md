# Meeting Notes Structurer

Transforms raw meeting notes, transcripts, or voice memos into clean, actionable summaries that people actually read and act on.

## What this skill does

Takes chaotic meeting notes and extracts what matters: decisions made, actions assigned, questions unresolved.

## How to use

Paste your raw notes, transcript, or recording summary. Say "structure these meeting notes."

## Output format

---

**[MEETING NAME]**
*[Date] · [Duration] · [Attendees]*

---

**Decisions made**
- [Decision 1] — decided by [who] ✓
- [Decision 2] — decided by [who] ✓

**Action items**
| Action | Owner | Due date |
|--------|-------|----------|
| [specific task] | [name] | [date] |
| [specific task] | [name] | [date] |

**Key discussion points**
Brief summary of what was discussed — context for people who weren't there. 3-5 bullets max.

**Open questions / parking lot**
- [Question that needs follow-up] → [who is investigating]
- [Decision deferred] → [when it will be revisited]

**Next meeting**
[Date, time, agenda items]

---

## Principles

- Action items must have an owner AND a due date — never just a task floating without both
- If a decision wasn't clearly made, flag it as open
- Ruthlessly cut discussion that didn't produce a decision or action
- Write action items as verbs: "Send proposal to X" not "Proposal"
- If the meeting had no decisions or actions, say so — that's useful information

## Handling unclear notes

If notes are too vague to extract clear actions, say so and ask for clarification rather than inventing detail.

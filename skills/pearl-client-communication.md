# Pearl Client Communication

Framework for all external client communications — proposals, updates, difficult conversations, and follow-ups.

## What this skill does

Ensures Pearl's client communications are consistent, professional, and effective regardless of who's writing them.

## Communication principles

**Before writing anything, answer:**
1. What does the client need to know?
2. What do we need from them?
3. What's the right tone given the context?

## Templates by situation

### Project update
Subject: [Project name] — update [date]

Hi [Name],

Quick update on [project]:

**Done this week:**
- [Specific deliverable]
- [Specific deliverable]

**Next week:**
- [What's coming]

**Anything needed from you:**
- [Clear ask with deadline if relevant]

Let me know if you have questions.

[Name]

---

### Delivering difficult news
- Lead with the situation, not the cause
- Take responsibility where appropriate
- Follow immediately with what you're doing about it
- Never bury the news in paragraph 3

---

### Asking for something
- One ask per message
- Explain why you need it (briefly)
- Give them a specific deadline
- Make it easy to say yes

## Response time standards

- Client emails: same business day
- Urgent issues: within 2 hours
- Proposals: within 24 hours of request

## What to avoid

- Sending long emails when a bullet list works
- Asking multiple questions at once
- Using "we" to avoid accountability ("there was a delay" not "we delayed")
- Ending with "let me know if you have any questions" — be specific

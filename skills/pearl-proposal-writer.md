# Pearl Proposal Writer

Structures and writes Pearl proposals that win — clear, specific, and focused on client outcomes.

## What this skill does

Takes a client brief or conversation notes and generates a structured proposal draft that sounds like Pearl, not a generic agency.

## Proposal structure

### 1. The situation (1 paragraph)
Prove you understood the brief. Mirror their language back. Show you were listening.

### 2. What we're going to do (the work)
Specific deliverables, not vague descriptions. 
- Not: "We will provide strategic consulting"
- Yes: "We will conduct 6 stakeholder interviews, synthesize findings into a 20-page report, and present recommendations in a 90-minute session"

### 3. How we work (process)
3-4 sentences. What working with Pearl looks like day-to-day.

### 4. Timeline
Specific dates or a clear week-by-week breakdown.
Never: "approximately 6-8 weeks"
Always: "Week 1: discovery. Week 2-3: analysis. Week 4: delivery."

### 5. Investment
Clear pricing. No surprises.
If there's a range, explain what drives it.

### 6. Why Pearl
One short paragraph. Not a list of credentials — a specific reason why Pearl is right for this specific project.

## What makes a Pearl proposal win

- It shows we understood their actual problem, not the surface ask
- It's specific enough that they can picture the outcome
- It's honest about what's hard
- It doesn't try to impress — it tries to be useful

## Tone check

Read the proposal aloud. If it sounds like a brochure, rewrite it. If it sounds like a smart colleague explaining a plan, you're done.

# Executive Summary Writer

Turns any long document, report, or meeting transcript into a crisp executive summary that busy people will actually read.

## What this skill does

Extracts the signal from the noise. Takes long-form content and produces a structured summary that answers: what happened, what matters, what's next.

## How to use

Paste your document, report, or notes and say "summarize this using my executive summary skill."

## Output structure

**[Title] — Executive Summary**
*[Date] · [Author/Source] · [X min read]*

---

**Bottom line up front**
One sentence. The single most important thing to know.

**Context**
2-3 sentences max. Why this matters and what prompted it.

**Key findings / decisions**
3-5 bullet points. Facts, numbers, outcomes. No opinions.

**Risks or open questions**
What's uncertain or unresolved. 2-3 items max.

**Next steps**
Who does what by when. Specific, named, dated.

---

## Principles

- Never more than one page
- Lead with conclusions, not chronology
- Numbers over adjectives ("revenue up 23%" not "strong growth")
- If it can't be cut, it wasn't important
- Jargon only if the audience uses it themselves

## Calibration

Ask before summarizing: "Who is the audience and how much do they already know?" Adjust density accordingly — a board summary is different from a team update.
